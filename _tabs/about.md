# Plex

Plex has been a giant inspiration for my homelab and the reason for it.

  

Plex is a software that allows you to host your own movies and tv shows in a nice graphical user interface and stream them to your wired or wireless devices. So at home, you can rip a copy of your favorite move from the disc and create a digital version of it, format it into whatever format you want, store it locally on your computer, attached media (usb thumb drive, hard drive, whatever), and point the Plex media server to that directory. From there you can watch it via the Plex app on your phone, streaming box (Roku, Apple TV, etc.), computer, or even straight to the smart tv itself.

  

Plex has many other features as well.

  

For me, Plex is an opportunity to host my own movies, tv shows, etc. I eventually want to get rid of Netflix, and Hulu who continue to raise their prices. The benefit to Plex is also that I am in control of what is kept versus what gets deleted. I know Netflix and Hulu both have gotten rid of movies and shows, and when I go to look for it, they no longer offer it.
